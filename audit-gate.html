Why this matters

1

Users assume HIGH means blocked

Every security tool users have ever encountered treats HIGH severity as a hard stop. Ginie's audit looks identical to those tools but behaves differently. The assumption is never corrected in the UI.

2

The audit is correct — the policy is silent

The findings themselves are accurate. Buyer and seller as observer-only on a deal contract is a real flaw. The problem isn't the audit — it's that "sandbox policy allows deploy" is buried in the log, not surfaced as a banner.

3

MainNet vs sandbox distinction is invisible

Nothing in the main deployment UI tells users that audit behavior differs between sandbox and MainNet. A user who tests on sandbox and sees HIGH findings deploy might incorrectly assume the same will happen on MainNet.

4

Proposed fix

Add a persistent banner on all sandbox deployments: "⚠ Sandbox mode — audit findings do not block deployment. All HIGH and CRITICAL findings will block on MainNet." One sentence closes the gap.
