Why this matters

1

You cannot tell from the UI

The deployment screen shows a green success state, compliance scores, and a real Contract ID. Nothing visually distinguishes a fallback deployment from a successful AI-generated one.

2

The contract structure is fundamentally different

In the escrow case: the requested model had buyer + seller as signatories. The fallback made arbiter the sole signatory — a completely different trust model. Buyer and seller have no consent path.

3

Scores and audit findings reflect the fallback, not your intent

The Compliance score of 95/100 and all 8 HIGH audit findings belong to the curated template. They say nothing about whether Ginie could have generated what you actually asked for.

4

Proposed fix

A single UI banner on the results screen — "⚠ Curated template deployed — AI generation failed" — would close this gap entirely. Users deserve to know which contract is actually running on their behalf.
